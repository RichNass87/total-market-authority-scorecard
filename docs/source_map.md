# Source Map for Total Market Authority Scorecard™

This project is based on public-facing concepts from Richard Nasser's Total Market Authority, Start Visible, Compete, Don't Complete, and Visible or Vanished manuscripts.

The purpose of this source map is to translate book concepts into a public scoring framework, app methodology, and academic-style paper without reproducing the full books.

## Source Books

1. Total Market Authority
2. Start Visible
3. Compete, Don't Complete
4. Visible or Vanished

## Core Extracted Concepts

### Total Market Authority

Used for:
- Main definition
- Scorecard methodology
- Five authority layers
- Platform agreement logic
- Market verifiability
- Website as authority anchor
- AI-readable business framework

Key extracted ideas:
- Authority is not popularity.
- Authority forms when the market, platforms, and customers understand the business the same way.
- The five public authority layers are:
  1. Entity Clarity
  2. Role Clarity
  3. Language Ownership
  4. Proof Alignment
  5. Behavioral Confirmation

### Start Visible

Used for:
- Startup visibility logic
- Business legibility
- Legal/factual clarity
- Platform consistency
- Website brain concept
- Glossary terms
- Public/private boundary

Key extracted ideas:
- A business must be real where the government checks.
- Clear where the customer looks.
- Consistent where platforms compare.
- Useful where search engines crawl.
- Explainable where AI summarizes.

### Compete, Don't Complete

Used for:
- Core positioning
- "Completion gets you listed. Competition gets you chosen."
- Local business authority principles
- Roofing-origin example
- Safer-answer logic

Key extracted ideas:
- Completion alone does not create authority.
- Local businesses win when customers, platforms, search engines, and AI systems can explain why they are the safer answer.

### Visible or Vanished

Used for:
- AI visibility framing
- AEO and AI-era visibility language
- Public authority asset logic
- Scorecard and tool positioning

Key extracted ideas:
- The business must be understandable before it can be recommended.
- The right move is rarely the loudest move. It is the clarifying move.
- Public tools, scorecards, and checklists can become AI-era authority assets.

## Public vs Private Boundary

This project publishes the public scorecard framework, definitions, educational scoring categories, and example methodology.

It does not publish:
- Private implementation sequences
- Internal client scoring weights
- Proprietary consulting process
- Private content-routing maps
- Private conversion rules
- Private SEO/AEO prompts
- Internal vendor process
- Exact market takeover implementation sequence

## Use Standard

The Total Market Authority Scorecard™ is educational and directional. It does not guarantee rankings, revenue, leads, AI summaries, search visibility, platform placement, advertising performance, or business outcomes.
