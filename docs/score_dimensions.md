# Total Market Authority Scorecard‚ĄĘ Dimensions

The Total Market Authority Scorecard‚ĄĘ measures whether a local business is clear, verifiable, consistent, and easy for customers, platforms, search engines, and AI systems to understand.

Total score: 1000 points.

Each category is worth 100 points.

## 1. Legal and Factual Clarity

Measures whether the business has a clear name, location, category, contact structure, and factual identity.

This category is based on the principle that a business must be real where official systems check and clear where customers look.

## 2. Entity Clarity

Measures whether platforms can identify the business as one connected entity across the website, Google Business Profile, social profiles, directories, owner profiles, and public proof assets.

## 3. Role Clarity

Measures whether the business is known for a specific role in the customer's decision, not just a generic service category.

Examples:
- Diagnostic-first plumber
- Inspection-first roofer
- Conservative-treatment dentist
- Documentation-first contractor
- Scope-control remodeler

## 4. Language Ownership

Measures whether the business uses specific, repeatable language that defines its method, standard, or point of view.

Generic language weakens authority. Defined language travels.

## 5. Proof Alignment

Measures whether reviews, photos, case studies, platform profiles, videos, posts, books, and service pages support the role the business claims.

## 6. Platform Agreement

Measures whether the website, Google Business Profile, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube, directories, marketplaces, review platforms, and author assets reinforce the same business identity.

## 7. Website Brain

Measures whether the website organizes identity, decisions, proof, definitions, FAQs, case studies, owner authority, and conversion paths instead of acting like a static brochure.

## 8. Machine Readability and Schema Discipline

Measures whether the business gives search engines and AI systems clear page structure, headings, metadata, internal links, structured content, schema, and visible facts.

## 9. Owner / Team Entity Strength

Measures whether the owner, founder, leadership, and team are publicly connected to the business standards, proof, process, and authority framework.

## 10. Behavioral Confirmation

Measures whether customers, reviews, questions, calls, engagement, and platform behavior confirm that the market understands the business the way the business intends to be understood.
