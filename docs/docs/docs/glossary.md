# Glossary

## AEO

Answer Engine Optimization. The practice of making a business, person, process, or idea clear enough to be selected and summarized as an answer by search engines, AI systems, and platform environments.

## Authority Asset

A public proof element such as a case study, review, photo, video, FAQ, book, owner bio, process page, or credential page.

## Authority Sentence

A one-sentence explanation of who the business helps, what decision it helps them make, how it helps, and what risk it reduces.

## Business Entity

The connected pattern of business name, owner, location, categories, profiles, methods, proof, and public behavior that platforms can recognize.

## Decision Page

A website page that helps customers understand a choice before they buy.

Examples:
- repair vs replacement
- wait vs act
- cheap vs durable
- DIY vs professional
- claim vs no claim

## Founder Entity

The public identity of the owner or founder when that person supports trust, expertise, standards, and business legibility.

## Market Verifiability

The standard that a business claim should be supported by public proof across multiple assets or platforms.

## Platform Agreement

When the website, Google Business Profile, social profiles, reviews, directories, ads, books, videos, and public profiles reinforce the same role in the market.

## Proof Stream

A steady flow of public evidence that shows process, judgment, context, and outcomes.

## Total Market Authority

A business state where the market, platforms, customers, and AI systems can explain the business the same way.

## Visibility Stack

The combined set of assets that makes a business findable and understandable: legal setup, website, Google Business Profile, reviews, citations, social content, proof, ads, schema, and owner entity.

## Website Brain

A website that organizes identity, decisions, proof, definitions, and conversion instead of acting like a static brochure.
